# Authorship

## Originator and lead

The ontology, the closure concept, the research direction, and the reduction into falsifiable
experiments were originated by **Vlad Ryzhkov** (independent researcher and machine-learning systems engineer; GitHub
[`data-mission`](https://github.com/data-mission)), who leads the program.

Origination here means the specific things a research program is: the initial stance (that language is a lossy
observable of a model's computation rather than its native ontology), the closure concept and its five-necessity
derivation, the decision to subject the vision to adversarial reduction, the judgment about which claims survived
and which were retired, and the decomposition of an open-ended theory into experiments that can each be run,
improved, or killed on their own. That work, and responsibility for it, is Vlad's.

## Contributors

This is an open program. Experiment runs, tooling, corpora, citation verification, and adversarial review are
all in scope ([CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)), and every one of them is a named, creditable contribution —
not a courtesy. The distinctions this program keeps:

- **Originator / lead** — above.
- **Experiment runner** — whoever executes an experiment to a verdict and lands the run in `/results` is credited
  for that run, in its `PROTOCOL.md` and in the pull request that updates [HYPOTHESES.md](HYPOTHESES.md).
- **Contributor** — tooling, corpora, or a fix, credited in the pull request and the file it touches.
- **Reviewer** — adversarial review is a first-class contribution here; a decisive objection, a confound a
  protocol missed, or a citation shown to be misattributed is credited as such.

A contributor is credited for what they did; origination of the program is not diluted into anonymous community
ownership, and a contributor's later work is not attributed to the originator. As the program grows, contributors
are added to this file and, where their contribution is substantial and ongoing, to [CITATION.cff](CITATION.cff).

## How to be credited

Do the work in the open: open an issue for what you are taking on, then a pull request with the result. Credit
follows the diff. See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for the evidence standards every contribution is held to.
