**Description:**

> This version supersedes the 2026-07-16 registered snapshot's headline finding. A post-verdict
> adversarial audit (Mission X, conducted within the same research program) found that the registered
> Arm-B-vs-Arm-C contamination separation (0.9% vs 10.3%, read as evidence that mechanical contraction
> injects contamination that instructed revision avoids) does not exist under a valid measurement
> instrument. The bidirectional NLI entailment metric used to score contamination fires on the
> requirement scaffold shared between a stale sentence and its corrected replacement, not on any real
> assertion of the stale conclusion — confirmed by a claim-grounded replacement instrument under which
> all three E5 arms carry approximately zero real contamination, including a single flagged item that
> triggers identically in both arms regardless of which arm's behavior it is scored against.
>
> Consequence: the registered refutation of H-RELEASE (the hypothesis that mechanical context rebuild
> is a safe substitute for instructed revision) is withdrawn. H-RELEASE returns to REOPENED. The
> original registered run artifacts are unchanged — this is a re-interpretation of the same banked
> data under a corrected instrument, not a re-run. A distinct, real, and separately disclosed defect
> (DANGLING_RULE, a completeness gap in 2 of the 11 originally-flagged items) is also reported; it is
> not contamination and does not affect the correction's conclusion.
>
> The correction is the program's own pre-publication catch: the same audit methodology that found
> this artifact in E5 also found and disclosed four further independent appearances of the identical
> instrument failure elsewhere in the program's revision-testing line, all resolving the same way. Full
> account: `E5-CORRECTION.md` in this deposition, and the standalone methodological finding in the
> program repository at `experiments/E8-instruction-breakpoint/coldstart-package/mission-x/FINDING-NLI-ARTIFACT.md`.
